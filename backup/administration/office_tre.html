Brief life Sketch

    Prof. Dr. Engr. M. Anwar Hossain is the First Vice-Chancellor of the Islamic University of Technology(IUT), Dhaka, Bangladesh, a multi-Governmental, multi-cultural University of the Jeddah-based 57-nation Organization of the Islamic Conference (OIC). He worked in this OIC institution since 1986 (then called ICTVTR), first as Professor and Head of Mechanical and Chemical Engineering Department, next as its Director General (then called as IIT) and finally as Vice-Chancellor of IUT till retirement in January 2003. During the formative stage of the institution, he was deeply involved in framing its rules and regulations, development of its course curricula, preparation of the laboratory and workshop layouts and selection and installation of the equipments and machineries, as the Team Leader of the Mechanical and Chemical Engineering Expert Group.

    Born on 05 January 1941 in Pabna, Bangladesh, Prof. Hossain obtained B.Sc. Engg. (Mechanical) degree in 1961 from Ahsanullah Engineering College ( now BUET ) under the University of Dhaka, Bangladesh after finishing his studies at secondary and higher secondary level from G.C. Institution and Edward College in Pabna. He was awarded M.S. and Ph.D. degrees from Cornell University, U.S.A. in 1964 and 1966 respectively. All through he had a brilliant academic record to his credit, particularly stood in order of merit First in I.Sc. (now H.S.C), First with Honours in Preliminary B.Sc. Engg. and Second in B.Sc.Engg. (Mech.) Examinations.

    Before joining IUT in 1986 (then called as ICTVTR), Prof. Hossain served Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET) for long 25 years from 1961 as, among others, Professor, Head and Dean of Mechanical Engineering, Member of the Academic Council, Director of Students Welfare (DSW), Founder Director of the Centre for Energy Studies and Provost of Hall of Residence. He was awarded Commonwealth Academic Staff Fellowship in 1974. He also worked in Mosul University in Iraq. He has about 40 papers published in national and international journals, primarily related to energy sector. As a mark of recognition of remarkable activities, his name has been included in the 5th edition of the WHO'S WHO IN THE WORLD published by Marquis, USA.

    Dr. Hossain was also associated in various ways and capacities with a number of other educational institutions, research organizations, commissions and public sector corporations of Bangladesh, like Bangladesh Agricultural University, Bangladesh Agricultural Research Council, University Grants Commission, Bangladesh Atomic Energy Commission, Bangladesh Planning Commission, Bangladesh Chemical Industries Corporation, Steel & Engineering Corporation, Bangladesh Standard Institute, Council for Bangladesh Institutes of Technology (BITs), etc. He was the Founder President of Bangladesh Society of Mechanical Engineers and was the last Chairman of the Board of Governors of BIT Rajshahi, now converted into a University, Rajshahi University of Engineering and Technology (RUET). He was also the Founder Head of Energy and Environment Centre of IUT.

    Dr. Hossain has a long association and deep involvement in various capacities with the activities of the Institution of Engineers, Bangladesh (IEB). To mention a few, he was elected as the President of IEB for the years 1990 and 1991 when major changes in the constitution of the Institution were initiated to make the apex body of the engineers of Bangladesh more dynamic and useful to the society. Earlier, he was elected as the Vice-President of IEB when he gave leadership in the PROKRICHI Movement of 1985-86 for the engineering community, as an outcome of which all the Engineering Colleges of Bangladesh were made operational as autonomous Bangladesh Institutes of Technology. Presently, he is a Member and Assessor of the Bangladesh Professional Engineers Registration Board (BPERB) and Equivalence Committee of IEB. In recognition of his outstanding performance and remarkable contributions, IEB presented him its highest award, IEB GOLD MEDAL, in 2004.

At the regional and international level, Prof. Hossain was a Member of the Executive Council of the World Federation of Engineering Organizations (WFEO) and its Education and Training-Committee, a Founder Member of the Executive Committee of the Federation of Engineering Institutions of Islamic Countries (FEIIC) and the Chairman of its Education Committee and a Member of the Executive Committee of the Federation of Engineering Institutions of South and Central Asia (FEISCA) and its Task Forces. He was also associated with the World University Services (WUS) in various capacities.

    At present, Prof. Hossain is contributing significantly as an active member of the National Road Safety Council of Bangladesh. He is also the Chief advisor of a very recently established non­profit Non-Govt. Organization called Bangladesh Center for Development Agenda (BCDA). Recently he worked as a member of the 6th National Pay Commission of Bangladesh to recommend to the Government the new pay scales and allowances for all the Government employees as well as for the employees of all the autonomous and semi-autonomous bodies in the public sector. Also very recently, he completed a specific task as a member of the Advisory Committee set up by the newly established Anti Corruption Commission of Bangladesh.

    Being imbued with the spirit of altruism, Dr. Hossain, a widely traveled person, has devoted himself to various social, philanthropic and extra-curricular activities. For many years, he was the President of Dhaka-based Pabna Samity (Association) established for the welfare of the people of Pabna living in Dhaka. He was also the Founder President of 61 Club of the 1961 engineering graduates of the erstwhile Ahsanullah Engineering College, for two terms when Dr. Rashid Professorial Chair and prestigious 61 Club Merit Scholarship were instituted in BUET. Recently, he was the President of North Bengal People's Welfare Samity. He was also a very good soccer player because of which he was invited to participate in the Combined Universities Football Camp of the then Pakistan in 1957.
